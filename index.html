My Chess Journey: From Beginner to 1500 Rating

December 29, 2024

Chess has been more than just a game for me - it's been a journey of continuous learning and improvement. Achieving a rating of nearly 1500 on Chess.com puts me in the top percentage of players, a milestone I'm particularly proud of. This achievement didn't come easily; it's the result of countless hours of study, practice, and learning from both victories and defeats.

The precision and methodical approach required in coding mirrors the strategic thinking needed in chess. I've noticed how my problem-solving abilities have improved in both areas. Each algorithm I write is like a chess match - requiring careful planning, considering multiple possibilities, and thinking several steps ahead.

One of my biggest inspirations has been Grandmaster Hikaru Nakamura, whose streams and games have been invaluable in my chess development. His ability to explain complex positions and strategic concepts has significantly influenced my understanding of the game. The recent triumph of Gukesh Dommaraju, becoming India's second World Chess Champion after the legendary Viswanathan Anand, has been particularly inspiring. As an Indian chess enthusiast, seeing Gukesh achieve this milestone fills me with immense pride and motivation to continue improving my own game.
